Evolution of Blockchain in the Web3 Ecosystem:

  • 1991 - Stuart Haber and W. Scott Stornetta had the vision of what we know today as blockchain. Their initial work was to create a cryptographically secured blockchain where no one could tamper with document timestamps.
  • 1992 - They updated their system to incorporate Merkle trees, improving efficiency by allowing more documents to be collected in a single block
  • 2008 - The history of Blockchain began to gain relevance thanks to the work of a person or group by the name of Satoshi Nakamoto. Nakamoto is credited as the brain behind blockchain technology. Although very little is known about him, it is believed that he could be a person or group of people who worked on Bitcoin, the first application of digital ledger technology. Nakamoto conceptualized the first blockchain in 2008, and since then, the technology has evolved and developed into many applications beyond cryptocurrencies.
  • 2009 - Satoshi Nakamoto released the first report on the technology in 2009, providing details on how this technology was well equipped to improve digital trust due to its decentralized aspect. On January 3, 2009, Bitcoin saw the light of day when Satoshi Nakamoto mined the first Bitcoin block, known as the genesis block. Nakamoto received a reward of 50 bitcoins for this achievement.

  • 2014 - Ethereum introduced smart contracts, allowing the creation of decentralized applications (DApps) on the blockchain.
  • 2015 - Hyperledger, an open source project to develop blockchain-based business solutions, was formed..
  • 2017 - The rise of Initial Coin Offerings (ICO) drove the adoption of blockchain in projects beyond cryptocurrencies.
  • 2020 - Blockchain technology was applied in areas such as logistics, digital identity, supply chain management and more
